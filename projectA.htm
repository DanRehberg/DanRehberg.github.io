Graphics programming offers a human interface to software, and extends from simple mimicry of the physical world to analogous office file system interfaces - and beyond. This is an extra level of interaction that exist between a user and a digital world. The projects in here offer solutions to some common concerns and more abstract concepts for producing special effects.

Ray tracing for occlusion allows for shadows to retain colors from the incident light sources. The benefit of ray tracing for general occlusions is removing screen space ambient occlusion artifacts and mishaps while allowing light colors to interact - which provide different hues of shadows in an environment. The limited tracing required for shadows ensures that runtime environments can utilize this form of raytracing especially well given the ease in reconstructing light volumes with minimal rays traced in a view - compared to other ray tracing visual effects such as reflections.
