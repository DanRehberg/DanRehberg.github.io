Physically-based animations go two ways: having armature limbs experience external forces and applying internal forces per limb to achieve an armature pose. This article reflects on the use of inverse kinematics to determine how internal armature forces (torques) should act to rotate joints to a desired pose, and finally, how these interactions can be influenced by the external environment. The results yield dynamic animations where armatures can influence and be influenced by a common configuration space. The discussion shows the iterative process to build an IK solver and then using it in a physically-based manner.
